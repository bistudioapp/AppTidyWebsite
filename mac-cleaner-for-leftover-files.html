Leftover Files

Find Mac app leftovers before they pile up.

AppTidy focuses on app-related files left behind after uninstalling: preferences, caches, containers, receipts, logs, and support folders.

What counts as an app leftover?

Many Mac apps store settings and support data outside the app bundle. Common locations include ~/Library/Preferences, ~/Library/Caches, ~/Library/Application Support, containers, logs, and receipts.

AppTidy shows why each file matched

Cleanup is safer when you can see the reason. AppTidy separates stronger matches from review items so shared folders, fuzzy names, and generic leftovers do not disappear without your choice.

Leftovers screen for orphaned files

The Leftovers screen can scan for abandoned caches and support folders that no installed app clearly owns. It stays review-only because developer tools and shared frameworks often use generic folder names.
